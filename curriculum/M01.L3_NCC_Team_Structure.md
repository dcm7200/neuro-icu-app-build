# M01.L3 — The NCC Team: Ice, Fire, and Everyone Around You

**Module:** M01 — Welcome to BNI Neurocritical Care
**Lesson:** 3 of 4
**Phase:** 1 — Orientation & Unit Foundations
**Estimated time:** 20 min
**Format:** Self-paced reading + reference card

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

1. Distinguish Ice and Fire teams by patient list, daytime attending, nighttime coverage, and downgrade pathway.
2. Describe the attending coverage windows across the 24-hour cycle, including when the night neurointensivist is in-house versus phone-only.
3. Identify every team role — residents, fellows, pharmacy, RT, nursing, EEG, social work, and therapy — and what each is responsible for.
4. Select the correct TigerConnect role for any scenario based on time of day and question type.

---

## Why This Lesson Matters

On your first admission at 2 AM, you will need to know exactly who to call and where to reach them. There is no time to guess. This lesson is your map of the team — not just names, but the logic of how coverage works, who owns what, and when to loop in each role. Every decision you make flows through this structure.

---

## Section 1 — Ice vs. Fire: The Two Parallel Teams

NCC runs two simultaneous teams covering the 4NBC and 4NNC pods. They are not interchangeable — they carry separate patient lists, have separate attending structures, and downgrade to different general neurology teams.

| | **Ice** | **Fire** |
|---|---|---|
| **Patient list** | NCC Ice | NCC Fire |
| **Daytime attending** | Ice attending | Fire attending |
| **Night attending** | Fire (via TigerConnect) | Fire (in-house until ~midnight) |
| **Downgrades to** | Blue team | Red team |

**What this means in practice:**

- Your patients follow their team's attending during the day. You always know which attending is running your list.
- At night, the Fire attending stays in-house until roughly midnight on most weeks. After midnight, both Ice and Fire night coverage is by phone — route through the NCC attending TigerConnect role.
- PCCM attending is **in-house 24/7** and is a co-management partner for all ICU-level pulmonary and critical care issues.
- When a patient downgrades, the destination team is determined by which NCC team they're on — Ice patients go to Blue, Fire patients go to Red. Know this before you initiate a downgrade conversation.

## Section 2 — Attending Coverage Windows

Understanding when the attending is physically present versus available by phone is essential for knowing how urgently you need to move on a problem.

| Window | Coverage |
|---|---|
| **Rounds** | 8 AM (or after grand rounds on Fridays) |
| **PM rounds** | At attending discretion |
| **4:30–5 PM** | Signout touch-base with daytime attending |
| **Evening (until ~midnight)** | Fire neurointensivist in-house |
| **After midnight** | Phone coverage — use NCC Attending TigerConnect role |
| **24/7** | PCCM attending in-house for all pulmonary/critical care issues |

> **Key principle:** Overnight phone coverage is still real coverage. Do not sit on a problem because you don't want to call. If you're asking yourself "should I call?" — call.

## Section 3 — When to Call the Attending

These are non-negotiable triggers. Any one of them means you call the attending — not after you've "worked it up," not after the next set of vitals. Now.

**Neurological:**
- Change in neuro exam
- Unexpected imaging findings

**Cardiovascular / Hemodynamic:**
- Hypotension on pressors
- New arrhythmia
- Troponin elevation
- Need for blood transfusion

**Vascular Access:**
- Need for central venous access

**Respiratory:**
- Worsening respiratory status
- Intubation watch
- NIV initiation
- Significant change in peak or plateau pressures

**Procedural / Surgical:**
- Pre-extubation (notify NCC attending AND PCCM)
- Pre-antibiotic start
- NSGY plan for emergent OR
- Any unplanned or major procedure

**Patient Safety:**
- Unexpected death
- Code Blue

**Everything else:**
- Anything you are unsure of
- Anytime you feel overwhelmed

There is no version of "I didn't call because I didn't want to bother them" that ends well. The attendings have said explicitly: they want to know.

## Section 4 — Residents and Fellows

**Neurology Residents:**
- 2 neurology residents per team (Ice 1/2 and Fire 1/2)
- Each resident sees up to 8 patients
- Schedule: 6 off-days per 4-week rotation, 4 per 3-week rotation
- Off-days are restricted: no clinic days, no Fridays, no weekends

**Neurosurgery Residents:**
- 1 NSGY resident per Ice team typically

**Fellows:**
- NCC fellow and epilepsy fellow rotate through
- Epilepsy fellow is your primary contact for EEG interpretation and new monitoring requests

## Section 5 — The Rest of the Team

You will interact with every one of these roles daily. Know what they own.

### NCC Pharmacist
**They own:** medication reconciliation review, HTS dosing, sedation management, antibiotic selection and stewardship, anticoagulation reversal, pentobarbital coma protocol, PRIS monitoring.

**Your job:** Bring sedation, HTS, AC reversal, and antibiotic questions to pharmacist during rounds — not via mid-day page. They are on rounds for a reason.

> Pharmacist owns pentobarbital coma protocol monitoring: q72h LFTs, propylene glycol surveillance. You do not need to independently track this — but you need to know it is happening.

### Respiratory Therapy (RT)
**They own:** ventilator setup and management, NIV initiation, suctioning protocols, ETCO₂ monitoring, patient transport for all intubated patients.

**Your job:** Loop RT in early for any vent changes, extubation planning, or transport. They are procedural partners, not order-takers.

### Bedside RN
**They own:** ACLS certification, whiteboard accuracy (your name, ASCOM number, contact method), and first-call for changes in patient status.

**Your job:** Verify your name and ASCOM number on the patient whiteboard every morning. A wrong number means a delayed call when things change.

### Charge RN
**They own:** Bed map, staffing logistics, Code Blue triage support.

**Your job:** Loop the charge RN in early on any bed/cohorting question. They are the operational quarterback for the unit — they know what's open, what's coming, and what's not going to work.

### EEG Tech / Epilepsy Fellow
**They own:** Continuous EEG setup (~30 min from order to leads on), interpretation, and reporting.

**How to reach:** Use the Ice+EEG or Fire+EEG TigerConnect role for your service. Reports route back through the same channel.

### Social Work / Case Management
**They own:** Complex disposition planning and advocacy for long-stay patients with disorders of consciousness.

**Your job:** Flag DoC patients early. The social work and case management teams need lead time — don't wait until the ICU LOS conversation is already urgent.

### Acute Therapy (OT/PT/Speech)
**They own:** Early mobilization, swallow evaluation, cognitive and functional assessment.

**Your job:** Flag DoC patients at admission for early OT/PT/ST involvement. Swallow evals gate oral medication and nutrition — get them ordered early.

## Section 6 — TigerConnect Address Book

This is your communication infrastructure. Know it before you need it.

| Role | When to use |
|---|---|
| **SJHMC NCC Ice Resident 1/2** | Daytime Ice neurology residents |
| **SJHMC NCC Fire Resident 1/2** | Daytime Fire neurology residents |
| **SJHMC Neuro Critical Care - Attending** | New admissions 24/7; urgent attending contact at any hour |
| **SJHMC NCC Fire** | Night attending — after midnight cross-coverage |
| **NCC Resident** | Cross-cover signout |
| **Ice+EEG / Fire+EEG** | EEG team for your service |
| **Pharmacy** | NCC pharmacist direct |

> **Rule of thumb for overnight admissions:** Use "SJHMC Neuro Critical Care - Attending" for all new admissions regardless of time. That role routes to whoever is on and in-house. Do not text the daytime attending's personal role for an overnight admission unless explicitly told to.

## Section 7 — Note Signature Discipline

This is operational, not administrative. Your progress note signature is how the team reaches you.

**Every morning, update your progress note signature to include:**
- Your ASCOM number for the shift
- Your daytime end time
- Your nighttime contact method

**Why it matters:** The bedside RN, charge nurse, and cross-covering provider will look at your note signature to reach you. If it says last week's ASCOM number, that call goes nowhere.

Your signature must also match what is on the patient whiteboard. Both get checked — both need to be right.
